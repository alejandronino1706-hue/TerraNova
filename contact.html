Intelligent Environment Monitoring

Through a digital platform and an interactive app, users will be able to view data on temperature, air quality, humidity, rainfall, wind, soil, and more. In addition, users will be able to report disasters or environmental problems, while experts verify the information and share recommendations.

How does it work?

Urban and rural sensors installed on bicycles, poles, vehicles, or stations measure variables such as temperature, pollution, humidity, rainfall, or noise. Satellites and CubeSats complement the data, detecting changes in terrain, vegetation, or climate. The information is displayed on an interactive map, indicating safe zones, critical areas, and environmental alerts. The community can record local events, and experts can validate or suggest solutions.

Types of sensors

  • Air

    MQ-135, BME680, SDS011 → Measure CO₂, toxic gases, and particles.

  • Climate

    DHT22, BME280, BMP280 → Temperature, humidity, and pressure.

  • Water and soil

    pH, TDS, DS18B20, YL-69 → Detect contamination, humidity, and temperature.

  • Vegetation

    NDVI and light sensors → Evaluate plant health.

  • Land safety

    Inclinometers, piezometers → Determine soil stability.

  • Natural hazards

    Seismic, thermal, and gas sensors → Monitor volcanoes or landslides.

    • Main applications

    • Detect urban areas with extreme heat and propose green areas or green roofs.

    • Identify pollution levels and create air quality maps.

    • Monitor vegetation and water health to prevent droughts or pollution.

    • Alert about natural hazards such as volcanic eruptions or floods.

    • Encourage citizen and scientific participation to improve environmental management.

    Our goal

    To convert data into concrete actions to improve air quality, protect ecosystems, reduce risks, and promote sustainable urban planning based on real evidence.

    Map

    Have a section of the app with a map where people can report unidentified problems or disasters, showing whether the information has been verified or not. In addition, have a section where experts can comment to help, and another where the experts' recommendations are stored, so that if it happens again in another place and a user searches for it, they can find that answer. Use geoscientific maps to locate safe areas for urban expansion (avoiding slopes, rivers, or protected forest areas).
